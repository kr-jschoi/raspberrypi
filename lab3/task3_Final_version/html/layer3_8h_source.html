20  1. packets are retransmitted through the ring if the packet needs
21  to reach other recipient(s) or handing the packet to layer 4.
22 
23  2. The address zero (0x00) is used as broadcast-address. The
24  addresses are assigned statically at start-up of the system.
25 
26  3. Whenever a layer 3 implementation receives a package, it checks
27  the destination address. If the address is equal to the address
28  gotten in initialization, the packet will be handed over to layer 4.
29 
30  4. Broadcast: the sender receives its own package, it knows that every
31  addressee has received the message properly and notifies layer 4 of
32  the successful sending operation.
33 
34  5. If the package is lost or corrupted on the way through the ring,
35  layer 4 needs to care about resending or ignoring the fault.
36 
37  6. Whenever the layer 3 implementation receives a message with its own address
38  or the broadcast-address set as source-address,it will discard the package.
39 
40  7. A CRC-check is not performed at the intermediate-nodes between
41  the sender and the receiver.
42 
43  8. If the destination differs from the own address, the ATMega can begin relaying
44  the read Bytes to the next node, while still receiving the remaining ones.
45 
46  9. An communication-error can be detected on the receivers end. This
47  behaviour is also possible for broadcast-message, but every node has to
48  check the destination address. If the receiving node sent the broadcast
49  message relaying must not be done.

51  10. Priorities: Packages that need to be relayed should be handled with
